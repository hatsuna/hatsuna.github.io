RPG Priest Simulator

In a completely stereotypical JRPG world, RPG Priest Simulator puts you in the atypical position of the village priest. You are beset by an endless stream of starry-eyed adventurers who have returned from their adventures with all sorts of ailments and afflictions. With the burden of student debt and moral obligation, you are armed with a physical physicianâ€™s manual (which was present at our jam table) and must cure as much of your congregation as you can.

RPG Priest Sim was the product of attending my first Global Game Jam, the theme for which was the word "ritual". As the only person in the group who was really comfortable with Unity at this point, working on the game was a real interesting shift for me. The first time I was at a game jam: a couple months short of a year ago; I had never used Unity or made a game before. At the time, I was mostly learning from someone who was vastly more experienced than I was. Now I was in a position of relative experience and had the rare opportunity to teach instead. Being able to reflect on my own personal growth (a significant time after the hectic weekend), as well how my own knowledge could benefit others, was quite the fulfilling experience.

Some of the aspects of RPG Priest Sim I was responsible for:

  • Prototyping core gameplay and interaction in Unity
  • Mouse-control and inputs for player interaction
  • Interaction and game logic with adventurers, tools, and specific afflictions
  • Hooks into database of dialogue, afflictions, tools, and tool combinations
